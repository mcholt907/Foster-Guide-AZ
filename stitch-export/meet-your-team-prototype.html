Who's in Your Case?

These are the helpful people here to support you on your journey.

Your DCS Case Manager

Your main point of contact at DCS. They manage your case day-to-day — writing your case plan, scheduling visits, and connecting you to services. By law they must meet with you in person at least once a month.

The Judge

Makes the big legal decisions about your case — including where you live. They approve your case plan and make the final call at every hearing. You have the right to speak at hearings — your voice counts.

Foster Parent / Caregiver

The adult responsible for your day-to-day care — in a foster home, kinship home, or group home. If you ever feel unsafe where you're living, tell your case manager, attorney, or CASA right away.

Your Attorney

Represents only you — not DCS, not your parents, not the foster family. They go to court with you, explain what's happening, and argue for what you want. Everything you tell them stays private.
